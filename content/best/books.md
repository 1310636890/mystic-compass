## Best Feng Shui Books by Level

### For Absolute Beginners

**1. Feng Shui for Dummies by David Daniel Kennedy**
The classic entry point. Covers Bagua, Five Elements, Flying Stars, and practical home applications. No prior knowledge needed.

**2. The Western Guide to Feng Shui by Terah Kathryn Collins**
Focuses on the Bagua Map method. Perfect if you want to start with room-by-room Feng Shui without diving into Chinese metaphysics.

### For Intermediate Practitioners

**3. Move Your Stuff, Change Your Life by Karen Rauch Carter**
Practical, no-nonsense Feng Shui for modern apartments and offices. Great for applying Feng Shui without expensive renovations.

**4. Feng Shui Your Life by Jayme Barrett**
Covers both home Feng Shui AND personal energy work. Good bridge between interior design and energy healing.

### For Advanced Study

**5. The Complete Idiot's Guide to Feng Shui (3rd Edition) by Elizabeth Moran**
Despite the title, this is surprisingly deep. Covers Classical Feng Shui, Eight Mansions, and Flying Stars in detail.

**6. The Feng Shui Bible by Simon Brown**
Comprehensive reference covering all major schools of Feng Shui. Keep this on your shelf for reference.
