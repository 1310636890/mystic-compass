The bedroom is the most important room in Feng Shui. You spend a third of your life there. If any room deserves getting right, it's this one.

Here's the thing most bedroom Feng Shui guides get wrong: they tell you to buy specific items. A rose quartz here. A bamboo plant there. But Feng Shui starts with **removing things**, not adding them. Get the basics right first, then accessorize.

## Rule 1: The Bed Rules Everything

Your bed position determines 80% of your bedroom Feng Shui. Get this wrong and nothing else matters.

### Command Position

You should be able to see the bedroom door from your bed — but not be directly in line with it. Diagonally across from the door is ideal. This gives you a sense of control without being in the "path of rushing energy."

### Solid Headboard

A mattress on the floor or a bed frame without a headboard is bad Feng Shui. The headboard represents stability and support in your life. Wood is best. Upholstered is fine. Metal with sharp corners is not great.

### Equal Space on Both Sides

If your bed is shoved against a wall with no space on one side, you're symbolically blocking your partner (or future partner) from having equal space in your life. Two nightstands. Equal breathing room.

### Nothing Under the Bed

Not storage boxes. Not suitcases. Not old clothes. Energy needs to circulate around you while you sleep. Stuff under the bed blocks that flow. If you absolutely must (tiny apartment), store only soft items — linens, pillows, blankets. Never shoes, electronics, or old paperwork.

## Rule 2: Ditch the Electronics

I know this one hurts. But the research backs it up.

No TV in the bedroom. Phone charges across the room — not on the nightstand. No laptop on the bed. The bedroom should be for two things: sleep and intimacy. Anything with a screen blurs that boundary.

If you can't remove the TV, cover it when not in use. A cloth, a cabinet door, anything that physically hides the screen.

## Rule 3: Mirror Placement Matters

Mirrors facing the bed are a classic Feng Shui no-no. The traditional belief is that mirrors bounce energy around while you sleep, causing restlessness. The practical translation: mirrors catch movement in your peripheral vision, which your brain registers subconsciously.

If you have mirrored closet doors facing the bed, cover them at night. A curtain rod + blackout curtain works. Or reposition the bed.

## Rule 4: Colors Set the Mood

| Color | Effect | Best For |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Soft blues, greens | Calming, restful | Most bedrooms |
| Warm beige, cream | Grounding, stable | Master bedrooms |
| Pale pink, peach | Romance, softness | Couples' bedrooms |
| Lavender, light purple | Spiritual, healing | Guest rooms, healing spaces |

**Avoid:** Bright red (too activating), dark gray/black (draining), and anything super saturated. The bedroom should feel like exhaling.

## Rule 5: Pairs, Not Singles

Feng Shui for the bedroom is about partnership — whether you're currently in one or open to one. Everything should come in pairs:

- Two nightstands (matching, not mismatched)
- Two bedside lamps
- Two pillows
- Art that depicts pairs or groups, not solitary figures

If you're single and want to stay that way? Still do this. It's not about manifesting a partner — it's about keeping that area of your life energetically open rather than closed.

## Rule 6: Remove Work, Remove Clutter

No desk in the bedroom. No exercise equipment. No piles of laundry. No stacks of old mail.

Every object in your bedroom sends a message to your subconscious. A desk says "work." A pile of laundry says "unfinished tasks." A stack of mail says "things you're avoiding."

If you live in a studio and literally can't separate, use a room divider or curtain to create a visual barrier between sleep zone and everything-else zone.

## Rule 7: What to Put in the Relationship Corner

The far-right corner of your bedroom (from the door, facing in) is the relationship corner in Feng Shui. Activate it with:

- **Rose quartz** — the classic love stone
- **Two of anything** — two candles, two crystals, two small plants
- **A photo of you and your partner** (if in a relationship) in a nice frame
- **Avoid:** single objects, dead flowers, anything broken

## Rule 8: Art That Supports Sleep

The art above your bed influences your subconscious for 8 hours every night. Choose wisely:

| Good | Bad |
|------|-----|
| Calm landscapes | Stormy oceans |
| Abstract in soft colors | Aggressive, chaotic abstracts |
| Single peaceful image | Gallery wall of 15 photos |
| Pairs of objects (two birds, two trees) | Single isolated figure |
| Nothing at all (also fine!) | Heavy mirrors, sharp metal art |
