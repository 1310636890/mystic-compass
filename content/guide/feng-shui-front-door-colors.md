## The Rule Most People Miss

Door color isn't just about what you like. In classical Feng Shui, the best door color depends on the **compass direction your door faces**. Each direction has an element associated with it, and certain colors either support or weaken that element.

If you don't know which way your door faces: open a compass app, stand inside your house looking out the front door, and note the direction.

## Door Colors by Compass Direction

### South-Facing Door (Fire Element)

**Best colors:** Red (any shade), orange, bright yellow, purple
**Avoid:** Black, dark blue (water puts out fire)
**Why:** South = fire element. Fire-element colors amplify your door's natural energy.

### North-Facing Door (Water Element)

**Best colors:** Blue (any shade), black, dark gray
**Also good:** White, metallic colors (metal feeds water)
**Avoid:** Red, orange, bright yellow (too much fire "evaporates" water energy)
**Why:** North = water. Water colors harmonize. Metal colors feed water energy.

### East / Southeast Door (Wood Element)

**Best colors:** Green (any shade), teal, light blue
**Also good:** Brown, earth tones
**Avoid:** White, metallic colors (metal "chops" wood)
**Why:** East = wood. Green feeds wood energy. Earth tones are neutral. Metal weakens wood.

### West / Northwest Door (Metal Element)

**Best colors:** White, gray, metallic (gold, silver, bronze)
**Also good:** Yellow, earth tones (earth creates metal)
**Avoid:** Red, orange, bright pink (fire melts metal)
**Why:** West = metal. Metal colors harmonize. Earth colors feed metal.

### Northeast / Southwest Door (Earth Element)

**Best colors:** Yellow, beige, tan, terracotta, any earth tone
**Also good:** Red, orange, pink (fire creates earth)
**Avoid:** Green, teal (wood depletes earth)
**Why:** Earth element doors do well with earth tones and fire colors. Green weakens them.

## Color Meanings Independent of Direction

If you're ignoring compass directions (or painting a door that doesn't face any specific direction), here's what each color signals:

### Red — Fame, Fire, Passion

Red is the most powerful Feng Shui color. It attracts attention, energy, and opportunity. In Chinese culture, red doors are associated with welcome and prosperity.

**Best for:** People who want more visibility, recognition, or social energy. Entrepreneurs. Public figures.
**Warning:** Too much red can be overstimulating. A dark red (burgundy, maroon) is more sophisticated than bright red.

### Blue — Wisdom, Water, Calm

Blue represents the water element — wisdom, flow, career, and calm energy. A blue door says "this is a peaceful, thoughtful household."

**Best for:** Career-focused people. Homes where calm is the priority. North-facing doors.

### Green — Growth, Wood, New Beginnings

Green is about growth, health, and family. It's the color of spring — new projects, fresh energy, forward movement.

**Best for:** Growing families. People starting new ventures. Health-focused households. East-facing doors.

### Black — Career, Depth, Water

Black is a water element color. It represents depth, career, and the flow of life. In Feng Shui, black doors aren't gloomy — they're powerful and grounding.

**Best for:** Career-driven households. People who value depth and substance. North-facing doors.

### Yellow / Gold — Earth, Stability, Nourishment

Yellow is the earth element. It's grounding, warm, and welcoming. Gold specifically is associated with wealth and abundance.

**Best for:** Families who want a warm, stable, welcoming home. Southwest or northeast-facing doors.

### White — Metal, Clarity, Precision

White represents metal energy — clarity, precision, and clean energy. A white door feels modern and crisp.

**Best for:** Minimalists. People who value order and clarity. West or northwest-facing doors.

### Purple — Spirituality, Wealth

Purple sits between red (fire) and blue (water). In Feng Shui, it's associated with spiritual awareness and wealth consciousness.

**Best for:** Creative households. Spiritual seekers. South-facing doors (as a variation on red).

## The "I Rent" Solution

If you can't paint your door: hang a wreath or door decoration in the corresponding color. The symbol matters more than the paint.

## Practical Note

Security doors, HOAs, and landlords may limit your options. If you can't change the door color, focus on the door's surroundings — a welcome mat, potted plants on either side, good lighting, and a clean, unobstructed path to the door. These matter more than the color.
