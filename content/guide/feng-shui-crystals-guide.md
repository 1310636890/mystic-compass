Here's what most crystal guides won't tell you: the crystal itself doesn't do anything. It's a focal point. A reminder. A physical object that tells your brain "this area is activated."

The value of a Feng Shui crystal is 10% the stone and 90% the intention you attach to it. A $5 citrine and a $500 citrine work the same way energetically. Buy what you like looking at.

That said — some crystals are better suited to specific Feng Shui purposes than others. Here's the actual useful information.

## The Core 6 Feng Shui Crystals

### 1. Citrine — The Wealth Stone

**Where:** Wealth corner (southeast), desk, cash register, wallet

Citrine is called the "merchant's stone" for a reason. It's the most traditional wealth-attracting crystal in Feng Shui. Natural citrine (pale yellow to honey-gold) is better than heat-treated amethyst sold as "citrine" (bright orange-brown).

**How to use it:** Place a small citrine cluster or tumbled stone in your wealth corner. If you run a business, put one near your payment terminal or cash box. Some people carry a small citrine in their wallet — same idea.

**Real talk:** Does it make money appear? No. But seeing it every day reminds you to make good financial decisions. That's the actual mechanism.

### 2. Rose Quartz — The Love Stone

**Where:** Relationship corner (southwest), bedroom, bedside table

Rose quartz is for love — romantic, self-love, and compassion. In Feng Shui, it activates the relationship area.

**How to use it:** Two rose quartz hearts (remember the pairs rule for relationships) in the southwest corner of your bedroom or living room. One rose quartz on your desk if you want to bring more warmth to your interactions with colleagues.

### 3. Amethyst — The Wisdom Stone

**Where:** Knowledge corner (northeast), meditation space, office, bedside

Amethyst is for clarity, spiritual growth, and protection. It's also allegedly good for reducing EMF exposure from electronics — the science doesn't support this, but if the belief helps you keep your phone out of the bedroom, it served its purpose.

**How to use it:** An amethyst cluster in your knowledge corner (northeast). A small amethyst on your desk for mental clarity during work. Under your pillow if you want more vivid dreams (don't knock it till you've tried it).

### 4. Black Tourmaline — The Protection Stone

**Where:** Front door area, near electronics, by the bed

Black tourmaline is for protection and grounding. In Feng Shui terms, it deflects negative energy — specifically from electronics, stressful people, and chaotic environments.

**How to use it:** Place one piece near your front door (inside, not outside). Put one near your WiFi router or computer setup. If you're dealing with a toxic coworker or difficult family member, keep a black tourmaline on your person.

### 5. Pyrite (Fool's Gold) — The Abundance Stone

**Where:** Wealth corner (southeast), office, business desk

Pyrite looks like gold. The Feng Shui logic is straightforward: surround yourself with symbols of abundance and you'll think more abundantly. Whether that manifests as actual wealth depends on what you do with that mindset.

**How to use it:** A pyrite cube or cluster in your wealth corner. On your business desk. In your home office. It's the "I'm serious about making money" crystal.

### 6. Clear Quartz — The Amplifier

**Where:** Anywhere — clear quartz amplifies whatever intention you place it with

Clear quartz is the Swiss Army knife of crystals. It doesn't have its own specialized purpose — it amplifies whatever other crystal or intention it's paired with.

**How to use it:** Place a clear quartz point next to any other crystal to "boost" it. Use it in the center of your home for general energy clearing. Point a clear quartz wand toward a specific area you want to activate.
