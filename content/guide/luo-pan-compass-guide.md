A Luo Pan (罗盘) looks intimidating. Twenty-plus concentric rings. Chinese characters you can't read. Symbols that look like they belong in an ancient temple.

But here's the thing: **for most Feng Shui purposes, you only need to use 2-3 of those rings.** The rest are for advanced practitioners doing things like date selection, astrological calculations, and landform analysis. You don't need any of that to find your home's directions.

## What Is a Luo Pan?

A Luo Pan is a traditional Chinese compass used specifically for Feng Shui. Unlike a Western compass that just shows north, a Luo Pan shows:

- The 8 cardinal and intercardinal directions
- The 24 mountains (15-degree segments that divide each direction into 3 sub-sectors)
- The Heaven Pool (the magnetic needle in the center)
- Various rings for advanced calculations (which you can ignore for now)

## The Parts You Actually Need to Know

### The Heaven Pool (Center)

The magnetic needle at the center. The red end points south (not north — this is the traditional Chinese convention). The crosshairs are for aligning the Luo Pan with the building or object you're measuring.

### The 24 Mountains Ring

This is the ring with 24 Chinese characters. Each represents a 15-degree segment. For example, south (180°) is divided into:
- 丙 (Bing) — 157.5°–172.5°
- 午 (Wu) — 172.5°–187.5°
- 丁 (Ding) — 187.5°–202.5°

For basic home Feng Shui, knowing the 8 cardinal directions is usually enough. The 24 mountains become useful when you're doing precise readings for door facing, bed placement, or stove positioning.

### The Outer Square Base

The Luo Pan sits in a square base. The sides of this square align with the walls or door frame of your house. The needle and rings then tell you the direction.

## How to Take a Reading

### Step 1: Stand Correctly

Stand about 3-5 feet inside your front door (or whatever you're measuring), facing out. Hold the Luo Pan level at waist height. Keep it away from metal — belt buckles, watches, phones can affect the magnetic needle.

### Step 2: Align the Square Base

The square base should be parallel to the door frame or wall. Use the crosshairs or sides of the base to align.

### Step 3: Read the Direction

Wait for the needle to settle. Turn the inner dial so the red end of the needle aligns with the south marker. Read the direction under the crosshair.

### Step 4: Record It

Write down the direction. For example: "Front door faces 175° (south, 午/Wu mountain)."

## The Most Common Use Cases

### Finding Your Home's Facing Direction

Your home's facing direction is the direction your front door faces (or the direction the building's "face" points — usually the side with the most windows or the street-facing side). This determines which Bagua map zones go where.

### Finding Your Bed's Direction

For personal Feng Shui, your bed's direction relative to the door and your personal Kua number matter. A Luo Pan gives you the precise measurement.

### Finding Your Stove Direction

In classical Feng Shui, the stove is the "wealth generator" of the home. Its facing direction matters for determining how wealth energy flows through your house.
