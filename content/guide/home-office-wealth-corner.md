## The Problem with Most Home Offices

Remote work sounds great until you're 6 months in and feel... stagnant. Same chair. Same walls. Same ceiling you stare at during Zoom calls. Your home office should be an engine for your career — not a holding cell.

Feng Shui has a specific concept for this: the **wealth corner** (Xun position on the Bagua map). It's the area of your space associated with prosperity, opportunity, and abundance — money, yes, but also career growth, recognition, and new clients.

---

## How to Find Your Wealth Corner

There are two methods. Use whichever clicks:

### Method 1: The Door Method (Easiest)

1. Stand at your home office door, facing into the room
2. The far-left corner from where you're standing = **wealth corner**

### Method 2: The Compass Method (More Accurate)

1. Open a compass app on your phone
2. Stand in the center of your room
3. Find the **southeast** direction — that's the Xun/wealth sector

> For most people, the Door Method is close enough. Don't overthink it.

## 5 Things to Put in Your Wealth Corner

Once you've located it, here's what actually works:

### 1. A Healthy Plant (Best ROI)

Living plants = growth energy. The classic choice is a **money tree** (Pachira aquatica) or **jade plant** (Crassula ovata).

⚠️ **Critical rule:** Dead or dying plants do the opposite — they drain energy. If you can't keep plants alive, use a high-quality fake one. It's better than a dead one.

### 2. Something Purple or Gold

Purple = wealth in classical Feng Shui. Gold = abundance. You don't need to redecorate — a purple notebook, gold pen holder, or a small gold frame does it.

### 3. A Moving Element

Stagnant energy = stagnant career. A small table fountain or even a screensaver with flowing water on a monitor in that corner works. The key is **movement**.

### 4. Your Vision Board or Goals List

This isn't woo-woo — it's environmental priming. When your goals are physically in your wealth corner, you see them every day. That repetition shapes your decisions.

### 5. A Citrine or Pyrite Crystal

If you're open to crystals: **citrine** (the "merchant's stone") and **pyrite** (fool's gold) are the traditional wealth stones. Even if you're skeptical: they're visually striking, they make the corner feel *intentional*, and that intention matters.

## Desk Positioning for Success

Your desk is your command center. Two rules:

1. **Command position:** See the door without being directly in line with it
2. **Solid backing:** Wall behind you = stability. Window behind you = energy drain. If you must sit with a window behind you, keep blinds closed during focused work

---

## Daily Rituals for Your Home Office

Feng Shui isn't one-and-done. It's maintained:

- **Morning:** Open windows for 5 minutes (fresh chi)
- **Midday:** Clear your desk of anything not related to your current task
- **Evening:** Close your laptop, clear your desk. Separate work from rest.

Still skeptical? Fine. Here's the non-mystical translation:

1. **Declutter your workspace** — less visual noise = better focus
2. **Add a plant** — proven to reduce stress and improve air quality
3. **Put your goals where you can see them** — visual reminders work
4. **Fix what's broken** — broken things create low-grade frustration
5. **Face the room** — psychological safety matters for sustained concentration
