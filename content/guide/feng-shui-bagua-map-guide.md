The Bagua map is the single most useful tool in Feng Shui. It's a 3×3 grid you overlay on your home to identify which areas correspond to which parts of your life. Career. Wealth. Relationships. Health. All mapped to physical locations.

Once you learn this, you won't need to guess where to put things. The map tells you.

## The Two Methods

There are two ways to apply the Bagua map. Use whichever works for your home.

### Method 1: The Front Door Method (Easiest)

1. Stand at your front door, facing **into** the house
2. Imagine a 3×3 grid over your floor plan
3. The row closest to you (your back to the door) = Knowledge (left), Career (center), Travel/Helpful People (right)
4. The far wall opposite the door = Wealth (left), Fame (center), Love (right)
5. The middle row = Family (left), Health (center), Creativity (right)

This works for most homes. It's simple and doesn't require a compass.

### Method 2: The Compass Method (More Precise)

This uses actual compass directions. You need a compass — either a phone app or a [real Luo Pan](https://www.fengshuiworld.com).

1. Stand in the center of your home with a compass
2. Find north
3. Align the Bagua with compass directions:

```
SE = Wealth       S = Fame        SW = Love
E  = Family       Center = Health W  = Creativity
NE = Knowledge    N = Career       NW = Travel
```

The compass method accounts for your home's actual orientation, which can shift the energy map significantly. But for most people, the door method is close enough to start.

## The 9 Zones Explained

### 1. Wealth & Prosperity (Southeast / Far-Left from Door)

This is about money, yes — but also about abundance, opportunity, and self-worth. What to put here:

- **Living plants** (healthy ones — dead plants drain wealth energy)
- **Purple** (the traditional wealth color) — a purple pillow, notebook, or frame
- **Moving water** — small fountain, image of flowing water. Water = money flow in Feng Shui
- **Citrine or pyrite** crystals (merchant's stone)
- **What NOT to put:** Trash can. Broken items. Dead plants. Bathroom items.

### 2. Fame & Reputation (South / Far-Center from Door)

Your public image. How others see you. Recognition. Awards. What to put:

- **Red** (fire element) — red candle, red art, red throw pillow
- **Things that represent light** — lamp, candle, bright art
- **Recognition symbols** — diplomas, awards, positive reviews, thank-you notes
- **Triangle shapes** (fire element shape)

### 3. Love & Relationships (Southwest / Far-Right from Door)

Partnership — romantic, yes, but also business partnerships and close friendships. What to put:

- **Pairs** — two of everything. Two candles. Two pillows. Two nightstands.
- **Pink or red** items
- **Rose quartz** (the love crystal)
- **Photos of you and loved ones** (happy ones!)
- **What NOT to put:** Photos of just yourself. Single objects. Dead flowers.

### 4. Family & Health (East / Middle-Left from Door)

Family connections, ancestry, and physical health. What to put:

- **Wood element** — plants (especially tall, upward-growing ones), wooden furniture
- **Green and blue** colors
- **Family photos** — multi-generational if possible
- **Column shapes** (wood element shape)

### 5. Center / Health (Center of the House)

This zone affects everything. Keep it clean. Keep it open. What to put:

- **Yellow and earth tones**
- **Square shapes** (earth element)
- **Open space** — resist the urge to fill the center with furniture
- **What NOT to put:** Clutter. Storage. Stairs (if avoidable). Center stairs drain energy.

### 6. Creativity & Children (West / Middle-Right from Door)

Creative projects, hobbies, and children (current or future). What to put:

- **White and pastels** (metal element)
- **Circular shapes** — round rugs, round art
- **Art supplies, musical instruments, creative tools**
- **Things that inspire play**

### 7. Knowledge & Wisdom (Northeast / Near-Left from Door)

Learning, study, and self-cultivation. What to put:

- **Books** — actual books you read, not dusty decoration
- **Blue, green, and earth tones**
- **A comfortable reading chair**
- **Quiet, calm energy** — this should feel like a library nook

### 8. Career & Life Path (North / Near-Center from Door)

Your work, your purpose, your direction in life. What to put:

- **Water element** — small fountain, image of flowing water, mirrors (reflect like water)
- **Black and dark blue**
- **Wavy, flowing shapes**
- **What NOT to put:** Stagnant water (still bowls of water). The water should move.

### 9. Travel & Helpful People (Northwest / Near-Right from Door)

Travel, mentors, networking, and "luck" in meeting the right people. What to put:

- **Gray, white, and metallic colors**
- **Travel souvenirs** — maps, globes, photos from trips
- **Contact list** (or business card holder) of mentors, collaborators, important people
- **Metal and circular shapes**

## How to Actually Apply This (Step-by-Step)

1. **Print your floor plan** or draw a rough sketch
2. **Overlay the 3×3 grid** using your preferred method (door or compass)
3. **Walk through each zone** and note what's already there
4. **Fix the problem zones first** — the areas that have clutter, broken items, or bathroom/trash
5. **Activate 1-2 zones** that matter most to you right now (wealth + career for most people)
6. **Wait 2-4 weeks** before making more changes. Let the first adjustments settle.

## Common Mistakes

- **Trying to fix all 9 zones at once** — you'll burn out. Do 1-2 at a time.
- **Buying a bunch of "Feng Shui products" before understanding the map** — most people don't need more stuff. They need less clutter.
- **Over-focusing on the wealth zone** — yes, everyone wants more money. But a clogged family zone or dead career zone will undermine your wealth gains.
- **Obsessing over exact compass degrees** — if you're off by 5 degrees, the Feng Shui police won't arrest you. Direction matters, but intention and clean energy matter more.
