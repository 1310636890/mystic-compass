Plants are the single easiest Feng Shui upgrade you can make. They're alive. They grow. They clean your air. And in Feng Shui terms, they represent the wood element — growth, new beginnings, and vitality.

But dead plants are worse than no plants. And the wrong plant in the wrong place can work against you.

## The 4 Rules of Feng Shui Plants

1. **They must be alive.** A dying plant drains energy. Water it or remove it.
2. **Avoid spiky plants in bedrooms.** Cacti, snake plants, anything sharp = "poison arrows" that disrupt sleep energy.
3. **Scale to the space.** A 6-foot fiddle leaf fig in a tiny studio is oppressive. A 4-inch succulent on a large console table looks lost.
4. **Round leaves > spiky leaves.** Soft, rounded leaves create softer, more welcoming energy.

## Where NOT to Put Plants

| Location | Why to Avoid |
|----------|-------------|
| Directly in front of the door | Blocks incoming energy (the "mouth of chi") |
| Bathroom (dark, no window) | Plants need light. A struggling plant in a bathroom = draining energy. |
| Center of the house | The center should be open. One small plant is fine; a forest is not. |
| Under a staircase | Low ceiling + low light = struggling plant = bad energy |

## The "I Kill Everything" Plan

If you can't keep plants alive:
1. Start with a **snake plant** (Sansevieria). It survives neglect, low light, and irregular watering.
2. Graduate to a **ZZ plant**. Also indestructible.
3. Graduate to a **pothos**. Trailing, forgiving, grows fast.
4. THEN try the plants on the list above.

A high-quality fake plant is better than a dead real one. Fake plants don't provide the same live energy, but a dead plant actively drains it.
