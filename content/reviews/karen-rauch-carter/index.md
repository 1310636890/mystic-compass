## The Bottom Line

Karen Rauch Carter occupies the sweet spot between "too academic" (Joey Yap) and "too basic" (random YouTube tutorials). 20% commission. Western teaching style. Practical home Feng Shui — not theory, not mysticism, just "here's how to arrange your living room for better energy."

The rating is 8.2/10. Not the highest in absolute terms, but for the audience she targets (Western beginners who want practical guidance), she might be the best fit.

## What She Teaches

Classical Feng Shui principles applied to modern Western homes. Her courses cover:
- The Bagua map applied to floor plans
- Room-by-room Feng Shui adjustments
- Color and element selection
- Clutter clearing (the #1 Feng Shui intervention, and she teaches it well)
- Exterior Feng Shui (garden, entrance, driveway)

The courses range from $97 introductory workshops to $2,500 certification programs. The mid-tier ($300-800) is where most affiliates see conversions.

## The Affiliate Program

In-house. Manual approval. 20% commission on courses and products. 30-day cookie. $50 minimum payout. Karen Rauch Carter also sells physical products (books, guides, tools), so there's cross-sell potential — someone who buys a course might come back for a book and you get paid again.

## How She Compares to Joey Yap

The key difference isn't quality — it's teaching style and depth. Joey Yap teaches Chinese metaphysics as an academic discipline. Karen Rauch Carter teaches Feng Shui as a life skill. If your audience wants to "become a certified Feng Shui master," send them to Joey Yap. If they want to "fix the energy in my home office," send them to Karen.

## Who It's For

**✅ Best for:** Western beginners, practical home content, "how to Feng Shui your [room]" articles
**❌ Skip for:** Advanced practitioners, audiences wanting Chinese metaphysics depth, international non-English audiences
