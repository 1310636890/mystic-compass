## The Bottom Line

Joey Yap is not just another Feng Shui platform — he's the biggest name in the industry. 4.5 million books sold. 183+ titles in 7 languages. Courses ranging from $20 ebooks to $5,000 certification programs. If you're going to promote one course platform in this niche, this is the one with the most brand trust.

The 10% commission looks low on paper. But do the math: if someone buys a $2,000 certification course through your link, you earn $200. Compare that to selling a $50 crystal at 35% ($17.50). **High-ticket wins over high-percentage every time.**

You need to make 11 crystal sales at $50 (35% = $17.50 each) to match one Joey Yap certification sale ($2,000 × 10% = $200). Different game.

## What Joey Yap Actually Sells

The platform breaks into six buckets:

1. **Online Courses** — Beginner to master level Feng Shui, Ba Zi (Chinese Astrology), I Ching, and Qi Men Dun Jia
2. **Books** — 183+ titles across Feng Shui, astrology, face reading, and metaphysics
3. **Live Seminars** — In-person events in Kuala Lumpur and occasionally abroad
4. **Software & Apps** — Feng Shui calculation tools, annual almanac apps
5. **Consultation Services** — One-on-one with Joey Yap's team of consultants
6. **Annual Almanac** — Yearly Feng Shui guide (massive seller every January)

The sweet spot for affiliates is the courses. Books are low-commission ($20 × 10% = $2). Seminars are hard to attend (in-person, Asia). But courses — especially the mid-tier $500-1,000 ones — convert well and pay decently.

## What It's Actually Like

I've gone through Joey Yap's beginner Feng Shui course. Here's the honest take:

The production quality is professional. HD video. Structured modules. Quizzes between sections. Certificate at the end. It's not some dude with a webcam — it's a real academy.

But it's dense. If someone is looking for "quick Feng Shui tips for my apartment," they'll drown in the first module. Joey Yap teaches classical Chinese metaphysics. He assumes you want to learn the real thing, not the Instagram version.

**One thing that surprised me:** the free content is genuinely useful. His YouTube channel has 500+ videos. The blog is updated weekly. Someone could spend months on the free stuff before buying anything — which actually helps affiliate conversions because the trust is already built.

## The Affiliate Program

Direct program. Auto-approve (instant). 30-day cookie. **$100 minimum payout** — this is the main pain point. You need ~$1,000 in course sales (10% = $100) to hit the threshold, or $5,000 in book sales to hit the same number.

If you're just starting out, the $100 minimum means you might wait 2-3 months for your first payout. Plan accordingly.

Joey Yap provides some promotional materials — banners, course descriptions, email templates. Not as extensive as some programs, but enough to get started.

## Who Should Use This Platform

**✅ Good for:**
- Affiliates with an audience serious about Chinese metaphysics (not just home decor)
- Content targeting "Feng Shui certification," "Ba Zi course," "learn Chinese astrology"
- YouTube channels that can embed free Joey Yap videos and link to paid courses
- Anyone who can do the high-ticket math ($200/sale × fewer sales > $17/sale × more sales)

**❌ Skip if:**
- Your audience wants quick tips and product recommendations
- You can't afford 2-3 month payout cycles
- Your content is mostly Pinterest/Instagram visual (courses don't pin well)

## The Smart Play

Don't lead with the $5,000 master certification. Lead with the $20-50 books and free YouTube content. Build trust. Then recommend the $500-1,000 intermediate course as the "next step." The conversion path is longer but the per-sale value makes it worth the effort.
