## 2026 Flying Stars at a Glance

The 9 Flying Stars shift positions every year on February 4th (Li Chun, the start of the Chinese solar calendar). In 2026, the configuration is mixed — some great stars in great positions, but also the year's most troublesome star (Star 5, misfortune) in a location that affects many modern homes.

Quick summary of 2026's star placement:

Star 8 (wealth) in South — the best news. Activate this sector with earth and fire elements.

Star 9 (future prosperity) in Southwest — secondary wealth star, great for long-term planning and relationships.

Star 1 (white, victory) in Southeast — excellent for students, job seekers, and anyone launching something new.

Star 5 (yellow misfortune) in Northeast — the one to watch. Keep this area quiet, add metal elements for suppression, and avoid renovations here.

Star 2 (illness) in North — another challenging position. Metal remedies are essential if your bedroom, kitchen, or main living area is in the North.

Star 3 (conflict/quarrel) in Northwest — not great for the patriarch or anyone in a leadership position. Fire-element remedies recommended.

The other stars are milder: Star 4 (romance/academics) in East, Star 6 (authority) in West, Star 7 (loss/theft) in the center.

## South Sector (Wealth) — Star 8

The South gets the best star of 2026. Star 8 represents current prosperity, financial growth, and material comfort.

What to do: activate with fire (candles, red decor, warm lighting) and earth (ceramics, crystals, yellow/brown tones). Fire creates earth in the productive cycle, so both elements work together here.

What works in practice: a citrine or pyrite cluster on a south-facing shelf. A red lamp. Even something as simple as cooking more often if your kitchen is in the South — the stove fire activates the wealth energy.

What to avoid: excessive water elements (black decor, fountains, mirrors facing the South wall) which can weaken the star. Also avoid too much metal (white, metallic objects) since metal drains earth.

If your front door is in the South in 2026: congratulations. This is one of the best door positions of the year. Keep the entrance clean, bright, and welcoming.

## Northeast Sector (Danger Zone) — Star 5

Star 5 (yellow misfortune) is the most challenging annual star. In 2026 it occupies the Northeast sector.

Fair warning: this is a problem for a LOT of modern homes because open-plan kitchens and living rooms often occupy northeast corners. If your kitchen, bedroom, or home office is in the Northeast, pay attention.

What to do: Star 5 is an earth star. Metal weakens earth in the destructive cycle. So the remedy is metal. A 6-rod metal wind chime in the Northeast corner. Metal decor. White, silver, or gold objects. A metal singing bowl or bell that you actually ring occasionally.

What NOT to do: NO renovations in the Northeast in 2026. No drilling, no hammering, no construction. Disturbing the earth energy of Star 5 amplifies its negative effects. Also avoid fire elements here — fire creates earth, which would feed the star you're trying to suppress.

If your front door is in the Northeast: keep this area quiet. No loud activities near the entrance. A metal doormat frame or white/gray doormat helps. And definitely no red welcome mats — that's feeding the problem.

## North Sector (Health) — Star 2

Star 2 (illness) lands in the North in 2026. This affects health, especially chronic conditions and recurring issues.

Same principle as Star 5: Star 2 is an earth star, metal suppresses it. The same 6-rod metal wind chime remedy applies. Metal bowls, metal frames, white decor — anything metallic or white in the North sector.

Specific concerns by room type in the North:
- Bedroom in the North: add a metal headboard or metal-framed art above the bed. Sleep with your head pointing away from North if possible.
- Kitchen in the North: keep it clean. A dirty kitchen combined with Star 2 energy amplifies health problems. The stove (fire element) naturally helps — cooking here regularly is actually protective.
- Bathroom in the North: problematic because water drains the energy. Keep the door closed, toilet lid down, and add a metal element in the bathroom.

If your front door faces North: hang a metal wind chime just inside the door, or place a white/gray doormat.
