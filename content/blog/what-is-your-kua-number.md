## What Is a Kua Number?

Kua numbers (also spelled Gua numbers) come from the Eight Mansions school of Feng Shui — one of the oldest and most practical branches. The idea is simple:

> Every person belongs to one of two groups — East Group or West Group — based on their birth year. Your group determines which 4 directions are lucky for you, and which 4 you should avoid.

Your Kua number ranges from 1 to 9 (with 5 splitting into male-2 and female-8). Each number corresponds to a specific element, personality, and set of lucky directions.

| 9 | East | Fire | Passionate, charismatic |

*(Note: There is no Kua 5 — it splits into 2 for males and 8 for females.)*

## Your 4 Lucky Directions

Every Kua number has four "auspicious" directions, ranked from best to least:

| Direction | Chinese Name | What It Does |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Sheng Chi** | 生气 | Wealth, success, prosperity — the BEST direction |
| **Tian Yi** | 天医 | Health, healing, vitality |
| **Yan Nian** | 延年 | Relationships, love, longevity |
| **Fu Wei** | 伏位 | Stability, calm, inner peace |

**How to use them:** Face your Sheng Chi direction when you work and sleep. Face Tian Yi when you're sick or stressed. Use Yan Nian for your bedroom to improve your relationship. Fu Wei is for meditation and rest.

That also means: when you buy a desk, put it so you face your Sheng Chi direction. When you position your bed, your head should point toward one of your lucky directions.

## Best Feng Shui Products for Your Kua Number

Once you know your number, you can pick items that resonate with your personal element.

### For Kua 1 (Water Element)

Your element is water, so you benefit from water symbols and dark colors. Good picks:

- **Water fountain** for your Sheng Chi direction — activates wealth energy
- **Black or deep blue decor** — strengthens your water element
- **Mirrors** — represent water and amplify positive energy

### For Kua 2 and 8 (Earth Element)

Earth types benefit from stability, warm colors, and grounding objects:

- **Crystals and stones** — citrine for wealth, rose quartz for love, amethyst for calm
- **Ceramic or clay decor** — earth-toned vases, pots, and statues
- **Yellow and beige accents** — strengthen your earth energy

### For Kua 3 and 4 (Wood Element)

Wood types thrive on growth, green colors, and living things:

- **Indoor plants** — especially broad-leaf varieties like money tree or peace lily
- **Wooden furniture** — natural wood desks, shelves, frames
- **Green accessories** — pillows, curtains, or wall art in green tones

### For Kua 6 and 7 (Metal Element)

Metal types need clarity, structure, and metallic accents:

- **Metal objects** — wind chimes, metal bowls, or metallic picture frames
- **White, gold, or silver decor** — strengthens your metal element
- **Round shapes** — mirrors, round tables, circular rugs

### For Kua 9 (Fire Element)

Fire types are energetic and need warmth and color:

- **Red accents** — pillows, candles, artwork with red tones
- **Lighting** — warm lamps, Himalayan salt lamps, fairy lights
- **Triangular or pointed shapes** — fire element shapes

## What About Your Unlucky Directions?

There are also 4 "inauspicious" directions you should avoid facing when working, sleeping, or spending long periods:

| Direction | Chinese Name | Severity | What It Causes |
|-----------|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Jue Ming** | 绝命 | ⚠️ Most Severe | Total loss, disaster |
| **Wu Gui** | 五鬼 | 🟡 Moderate | Gossip, betrayal, fire |
| **Liu Sha** | 六煞 | 🟡 Moderate | Scandals, legal issues |
| **Huo Hai** | 祸害 | 🔵 Least Severe | Minor accidents, arguments |

**You don't need to panic about these.** Just avoid facing them directly when you sit for long periods. Most homes and offices are oriented in ways that make it easy to pick a good direction.

## How to Apply Kua Numbers to Your Home

Here's the practical checklist — once you have your Kua number from the tool:

1. **Your front door:** Ideally faces one of your lucky directions
2. **Your bed:** Head points toward Sheng Chi (wealth) or Yan Nian (relationships)

5. **Colors in each room:** Match your personal element for extra benefit
